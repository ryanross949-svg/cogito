About MENDEL

Methodology and design principles.

MENDEL is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the thinking patterns of geneticists and systems biologists. It trains you to see DNA as code, understand how traits propagate, and map the algorithmic replication of life.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Replication require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • ALPHABET — The 4 base pairs and DNA structure
  • ENCODE — Transcription & translation (DNA to protein)
  • DOMINANCE — Mendelian inheritance & Punnett squares
  • MUTATE — Point mutations & copying errors
  • CROSSING — Recombination & meiosis
  • DRIFT — Genetic drift & population bottlenecks
  • SELECTION — Natural selection on allele frequencies
  • REGULATE — Epigenetics & gene expression
  • REPLICATE — The mechanics of DNA copying
  • HORIZON — Horizontal gene transfer & viruses

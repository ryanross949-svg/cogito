About TETLOCK

Methodology and design principles.

TETLOCK is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the forecasting and calibration patterns used by superforecasters, based on the research of Philip Tetlock. It is not a textbook. It is a workout.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality. Forgetting curve combat.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition. You must produce the answer, not just identify it.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules. Forced discrimination strengthens memory vs. blocked practice.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project ORACLE require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • FOXY — Fox vs Hedgehog thinking
  • CALIB — Probabilistic calibration
  • BRIER — Brier scores and measurement
  • DECOMP — Fermi-izing the future
  • BASE — Base rates and the outside view
  • BAYES — Bayesian updating
  • DRAGON — Cognitive biases and dragon effects
  • PACK — The wisdom of crowds
  • HORIZON — Time horizons and forecast decay
  • SUPRA — The superforecaster mindset

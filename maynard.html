About MAYNARD

Methodology and design principles.

MAYNARD SMITH is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the strategic thinking patterns used in evolutionary game theory. It is the missing link between NASH (Strategy) and DARWIN (Evolution).

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project DARWIN require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • ESS — Evolutionarily Stable Strategies
  • HAWK — Hawk-Dove Game
  • FREQ — Frequency-Dependent Selection
  • FITNESS — Direct & Inclusive Fitness
  • MUTATE — Mutations and Invasion
  • REPLICATOR — Replicator Dynamics
  • KIN — Kin Selection & Hamilton's Rule
  • SEX — Sexual Selection & Fisher's Principle
  • COOP — Reciprocal Altruism & Tit-for-Tat
  • EVOLVE — Coevolution & Niche Construction

About MACH

Methodology and design principles.

MACH is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the spatial reasoning and physical intuition used by orbital mechanics and engineers. It trains you to predict motion, leverage forces, and navigate systems under constraints.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Orbital require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • INERTIA — The First Law. Resistance to change.
  • FORCE — F=ma. Cause and effect in acceleration.
  • ACTION — 3rd Law. Equal and opposite reactions.
  • ENERGY — Work, kinetic/potential, conservation.
  • MOMENTUM — Impulse, collisions, continuity.
  • TORQUE — Rotational motion and leverage.
  • GRAVITY — Inverse square law, orbits.
  • THERMO — Heat, entropy, work over time.
  • WAVE — Frequency, resonance, interference.
  • RELATIV — Time dilation, frames of reference.

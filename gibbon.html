About GIBBON

Methodology and design principles.

GIBBON is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the macro-level pattern recognition used by historians and systems architects. It trains you to see the structural rot that precedes collapse in empires, corporations, and codebases.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Collapse require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • EMPIRE — The cost of empire, over-extension, and negative ROI of complexity
  • BLOAT — Bureaucratic rot, Parkinson's Law, and principal-agent problems
  • CASCADE — Non-linear collapse, feedback loops, and domino effects
  • DECENT — Decentralization, hub-and-spoke vs. mesh, and resilience
  • SIGNAL — Information decay, filtered feedback, and "lying to the king"
  • ENTROPY — Systems entropy, maintenance costs, and technical debt
  • ELITE — Elite overproduction, infighting, and structural-demographic theory
  • FRINGE — Innovation at the periphery, core vs. edge dynamics
  • CULT — Cultural shift, loss of founding ethos, and institutional drift
  • REBIRTH — Renewal, dark ages as resets, and phoenix cycles

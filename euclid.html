About EUCLID

Methodology and design principles.

EUCLID is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the spatial and geometric reasoning patterns used by architects, topologists, and physicists. It trains you to manipulate objects and space in your mind, a fundamentally different cognitive mode than pure logic or estimation.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Tessellation require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • AXIOM — The axiomatic method, postulates, and deduction
  • CONGRUENCE — Rigid motions, SAS, SSS, and transformations
  • SIMILARITY — Scaling, ratios, and Thales\' theorem
  • AREA — Dissection, Cavalieri\'s principle, and Pythagoras
  • SYMMETRY — Reflections, rotations, and group theory
  • OPTIMA — Isoperimetric inequality, shortest paths
  • TILING — Tessellations, platonic solids, Euler\'s formula
  • CONIC — Slicing cones, ellipses, and parabolas
  • TOPOLOGY — Rubber sheet geometry, genus, and knots
  • NON-EUCLID — Spherical and hyperbolic geometry

About FERMI

Methodology and design principles.

FERMI is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the quantitative thinking patterns used by physicists and order-of-magnitude engineers. It trains you to sanity-check any number, spot theoretical limits, and navigate the world via scales of ten.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Critical Mass require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • ORDERS — Orders of magnitude, powers of ten, scientific notation
  • BOUND — Theoretical limits (speed of light, Carnot, Shannon)
  • ESTIMATE — Fermi problems (piano tuners, atoms in a room)
  • RATIO — Dimensional analysis, scaling laws, square-cube law
  • RATE — Exponential growth, Moore's law, half-life
  • ENERGY — Power vs energy, thermodynamics, energy density
  • PROB — Probability, Bayes theorem, normal distributions
  • TRADE — Optimization, rocket equation, cost functions
  • ENTROPY — Information theory, 2nd law of thermodynamics
  • EMERGE — Chaos theory, phase transitions, criticality

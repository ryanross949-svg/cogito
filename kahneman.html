About KAHNEMAN

Methodology and design principles.

KAHNEMAN is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the awareness of cognitive biases and heuristics. It trains you to recognize the hardware bugs in human reasoning before they ruin your strategies.

// Core Pedagogy

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Blind Spot require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • SYSTEMS — System 1 vs. System 2 thinking
  • ANCHOR — The anchoring effect
  • SUNK — Sunk cost fallacy
  • CONFIRM — Confirmation bias
  • AVAIL — Availability heuristic
  • LOSS — Loss aversion & Prospect theory
  • BASE — Base rate neglect
  • NARR — Narrative fallacy & Hindsight bias
  • OVER — Overconfidence effect
  • DEBIAS — Debiasing techniques

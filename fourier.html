About FOURIER

Methodology and design principles.

FOURIER is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the mental models of signal processing, frequency analysis, and wave decomposition. It trains an entirely different mode of visual and mathematical thinking.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality. Forgetting curve combat.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition. You must produce the answer, not just identify it.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules. Forced discrimination strengthens memory vs. blocked practice.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project SIREN require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • TIME — Time domain, periodicity, and sine waves
  • FREQ — Frequency domain, Hertz, and pitch
  • PHASE — Phase shifts, delay, and interference
  • AMP — Amplitude, energy, and power
  • HARM — Harmonics, overtones, and timbre
  • TRANS — The Fourier Transform (unmixing the wave)
  • FILTER — Low-pass, high-pass, and equalization
  • CONVOL — Convolution and impulse response
  • SAMPLE — Sampling, aliasing, and Nyquist
  • COMP — Compression, FFT, and psychoacoustics

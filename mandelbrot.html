About MANDELBROT

Methodology and design principles.

MANDELBROT is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the risk-thinking patterns used by quantitative risk-takers (Taleb, Mandelbrot, Kelly). It trains you to recognize that the real world (markets, wars, wealth) does not follow a normal distribution, and teaches you to prepare for extreme, catastrophic outliers rather than averages.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Black Swan require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • WILD — The Wild vs. The Mild (Mediocristan vs. Extremistan)
  • PARETO — Power laws and the 80/20 scaling rule
  • FAT — Fat tails and infinite variance
  • BLACKSWAN — Improbable, high-impact, retrospective predictability
  • SCALE — Fractals, scale invariance, and self-similarity
  • LUDIC — The casino fallacy and model truncation
  • ANTIFRAG — Gaining from volatility and disorder
  • RUIN — Absorbing barriers and avoiding zero
  • HISTORY — Silent evidence and the narrative fallacy
  • ASYMM — Convexity, optionality, and asymmetric payoffs

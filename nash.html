About NASH

Methodology and design principles.

NASH is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the strategic thinking patterns used in advanced game theory, modeled after MIT OpenCourseWare 14.12. It is not a textbook. It is a workout.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality. Forgetting curve combat.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition. You must produce the answer, not just identify it.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules. Forced discrimination strengthens memory vs. blocked practice.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Spectrum require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • MATRIX — Normal form games, players, strategies, and payoffs
  • DOMINANCE — Iterated elimination of strictly dominated strategies
  • EQUILIBRIUM — Nash Equilibrium and mutual best responses
  • MIXED — Mixed strategies and zero-sum games
  • EXTENSIVE — Game trees, backward induction, and credible threats
  • REPEATED — Iterated games, trigger strategies, and the Folk Theorem
  • BAYESIAN — Incomplete information and Bayesian updating
  • MECHANISM — Auctions, VCG, and incentive-compatible design
  • SIGNALLING — Separating vs. pooling equilibria
  • EVOLUTIONARY — Evolutionary Stable Strategies (ESS)

About SENECA

Methodology and design principles.

SENECA is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the decision-making frameworks of Stoic philosophy. It turns ancient wisdom into actionable mental models for modern stress, risk, and leadership.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Inner Citadel require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • CONTROL — The Dichotomy of Control (Epictetus)
  • VIEW — Cognitive Distancing and Objective Impression
  • VOLATILE — Negative Visualization (Premeditatio Malorum)
  • FATE — Amor Fati (Loving what happens)
  • MORTAL — Memento Mori (Remember you must die)
  • VIRTUE — The Four Cardinal Virtues
  • TIME — Presence and Anti-Distraction
  • RISK — Asymmetry of Risk and Anti-Fragility
  • EMOTION — Passions vs. Protopassions
  • CITADEL — The Inner Fortress (Marcus Aurelius)

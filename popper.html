About POPPER

Methodology and design principles.

POPPER is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the epistemological frameworks of Karl Popper and Thomas Kuhn. It trains you to hold beliefs tentatively, seek disconfirming evidence, and update your mental models when reality pushes back.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Open Society require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • BLACK SWAN — Falsifiability and the limits of induction
  • DEMARCATION — Science vs. pseudoscience
  • PARADIGM — Normal science and revolutionary shifts
  • TENTATIVE — Corroboration vs. truth
  • DANGER — Confirmation bias and positive instances
  • INDUCTIVE — Hume's problem of induction
  • VERISIMILITUDE — Truthlikeness and getting closer to reality
  • CRITICAL — Critical rationalism and piecemeal engineering
  • ORGANIZED — Organized skepticism and the open society
  • UPDATE — Falsification in practice and Bayesian updating

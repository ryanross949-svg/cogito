About MAESTRO

Methodology and design principles.

MAESTRO is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the strategic thinking patterns required to master complex systems, feedback loops, and architectural scaling. It is not a tutorial. It is a workout.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality. Forgetting curve combat.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition. You must produce the answer, not just identify it.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules. Forced discrimination strengthens memory vs. blocked practice.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Zenith require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • FOUNDATIONS — Stocks, flows, boundaries, open vs closed systems
  • FEEDBACK — Reinforcing and balancing loops, runaway growth
  • CAUSAL — Link polarity, delays, and system oscillation
  • BOTTLENECK — Theory of Constraints, throughput, and drum-buffer-rope
  • EMERGENCE — Simple rules leading to complex macro-behavior
  • NETWORK — Topology, Metcalfe's Law, and robust-yet-fragile hubs
  • SCALING — Kleiber's Law, biological vs urban scaling curves
  • RESILIENCE — Redundancy, optionality, and antifragility
  • NONLINEAR — Bifurcation, attractors, and chaos theory
  • ARCHITECT — Abstraction layers, interfaces, and Conway's Law

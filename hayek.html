About HAYEK

Methodology and design principles.

HAYEK is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the thinking patterns of Austrian economics and complex systems theory. It trains you to see spontaneous order, price signals, and the asymmetry of knowledge in decentralized networks.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Invisible Hand require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • SIGNALS — Price as information transmission
  • KNOWLEDGE — Division and asymmetry of knowledge
  • ORDER — Spontaneous order vs central planning
  • COMMONS — Tragedy of the commons, externalities
  • ASYMMETRY — Information asymmetry, lemons markets
  • DISCOVERY — Competition as a discovery procedure
  • COORDINATION — Coordination failures, moral hazard
  • MONEY — Sound money, Cantillon effects
  • SCALE — Economies of scale, monopolies
  • EVOLUTION — Cultural and institutional evolution

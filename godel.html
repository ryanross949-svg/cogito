About GÖDEL

Methodology and design principles.

GÖDEL is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the logical thinking patterns used by Kurt Gödel, Alan Turing, and other foundational logicians. It is not a textbook. It is a workout.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project FOUNDATION require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • LIAR — Self-reference and paradoxes
  • AXIOM — Formal systems and axioms
  • GODEL — First Incompleteness Theorem
  • CONSIS — Second Incompleteness Theorem
  • INFIN — Cantor and diagonalization
  • HALT — Turing machines and computability limits
  • TRUTH — Tarski and undefinability
  • MODEL — Non-standard models
  • PROOF — Provability logic
  • BOUND — Philosophy of math and epistemology

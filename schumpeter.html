About SCHUMPETER

Methodology and design principles.

SCHUMPETER is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the strategic thinking patterns of innovation economics. It trains the brain to see how the destruction of old systems is not a bug of capitalism, but the essential mechanism of progress.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality. Forgetting curve combat.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition. You must produce the answer, not just identify it.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules. Forced discrimination strengthens memory vs. blocked practice.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project PHOENIX require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • GALE — The Gale of Destruction (Why perfect equilibrium is stagnation)
  • PARADIGM — Technological Paradigm Shifts (Kuhnian structure applied to economics)
  • MONOPOLY — The Necessity of Monopoly (Why perfect competition is a trap)
  • ENTREPRENEUR — The Entrepreneur as Disruptor (Innovator vs. Manager)
  • CYCLE — Long Waves and Business Cycles (Kondratiev waves)
  • CREDIT — Credit and Capital Creation (Funding the future)
  • OBSOLESCENCE — Planned and Natural Obsolescence (The death of the old)
  • RIGIDITY — The Rigidity of Incumbents (The Innovator's Dilemma)
  • INSTITUTION — Institutional Adaptation (Law vs. Tech)
  • PROGRESS — The Engine of Progress (Destruction as wealth creation)

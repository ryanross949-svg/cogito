About ALTSHULLER

Methodology and design principles.

ALTSHULLER is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the inventive thinking patterns of TRIZ (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving). It turns the "magic" of creativity into a hard, algorithmic science.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Invention require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • CONTRADICT — Physical vs Technical contradictions, breaking trade-offs
  • IFR — Ideal Final Result, systems evolving to disappear
  • RESOLVE — Separation principles (Space, Time, Condition)
  • PRINCIPLES — The 40 Inventive Principles (Segmentation, Asymmetry)
  • DYNAMISM — Increasing flexibility and controllability
  • MATR — The Contradiction Matrix and 39 parameters
  • EVOLUTION — Trends of technical system evolution (S-curves)
  • SUBSTANCE — Su-Field modeling (Substance-Field analysis)
  • NATURE — Using natural resources and effects
  • INVENT — ARIZ (Algorithm of Inventive Problem Solving)

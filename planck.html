About PLANCK

Methodology and design principles.

PLANCK is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the probabilistic thinking patterns of quantum mechanics. It trains you to accept that reality at its fundamental level is non-deterministic, and that the act of observation changes the outcome.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Quantum Leap require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • OBSERVER — The observer effect: measurement alters state
  • SUPERPOSE — Superposition: multiple states simultaneously
  • UNCERTAIN — Heisenberg uncertainty: limits of knowledge
  • ENTANGLE — Quantum entanglement: non-local correlations
  • TUNNEL — Quantum tunneling: overcoming barriers probabilistically
  • DECOHERE — Decoherence: transition from quantum to classical
  • PROBABLE — Quantum probability: interference of outcomes
  • QUBIT — Information theory: bits vs qubits
  • ZENO — Quantum Zeno effect: observation freezing systems
  • COGNITION — Quantum cognition: applying quantum math to human thought

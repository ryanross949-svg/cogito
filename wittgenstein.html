About WITTGENSTEIN

Methodology and design principles.

WITTGENSTEIN is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the semantic clarity used by logicians and philosophers of language. It trains you to recognize when a problem is actually just a confusion of language, and how the words we use constrain the realities we can perceive.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Silence require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • LIMITS — The limits of language are the limits of thought
  • GAMES — Meaning as use; forms of life
  • FAMILY — Family resemblance vs. strict essence
  • RULES — Rule-following and interpretation
  • PRIVATE — The private language argument
  • FORM — Forms of life as the bedrock of meaning
  • SHOWING — Showing vs. Saying; the inexpressible
  • GRAMMAR — Grammar as logic, not empirical fact
  • SILENCE — Ethics, aesthetics, and the mystical
  • THERAPY — Philosophy as therapy; untangling knots

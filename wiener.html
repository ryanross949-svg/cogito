Feedback Lab

Cybernetics is the science of steering by feedback. A controller measures the gap between where the system is and where you want it, then corrects in proportion. Turn up the gain for a faster response — but push too hard and the loop overshoots and oscillates. Find the sweet spot.

About WIENER

Methodology and design principles.

WIENER is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the feedback-loop thinking patterns used by control engineers and systems biologists. It trains you to design self-correcting loops in everything from engineering to personal habits.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Steersman require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • SERVO — The Servomechanism (Continuous error-feedback, hitting moving targets)
  • ERROR — Error Correction (Difference between state and goal, gain)
  • DAMP — Damping & Oscillation (Overcorrection, overshoot, critical damping)
  • LAG — Delays & Phase Shift (Time delays in feedback loops, latency)
  • STEADY — Steady-State Error (Integral control, eliminating offset)
  • RATE — Derivative Action (Predictive control, anticipating error)
  • PID — The PID Controller (Combining P, I, D; tuning the loop)
  • HOMEOS — Homeostasis & Biology (Biological cybernetics, blood sugar)
  • GOAL — Goal-Seeking Behavior (Teleology, Ashby's Law of Requisite Variety)
  • ADAPT — Adaptive Control (Updating models, learning loops, meta-feedback)

About DIFFIE

Methodology and design principles.

DIFFIE is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the adversarial thinking patterns used by cryptographers and security architects. It trains you to verify truth without revealing data, and to build systems that survive in a room full of eavesdroppers and malicious actors.

// Core Pedagogy

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Zero-Trust require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • AXIOM — Perfect secrecy, One-Time Pads, and Kerckhoffs's principle
  • HASH — Cryptographic hashes, integrity, and Merkle trees
  • EXCHANGE — Diffie-Hellman, discrete log, and key agreement
  • ASYMM — Public key cryptography, RSA, and trapdoor functions
  • SIGN — Digital signatures, non-repudiation, and authentication
  • ZERO — Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs)
  • ENTROPY — Random number generation and seed security
  • TRUST — PKI, Certificate Authorities, and Web of Trust
  • BYZANTINE — Byzantine fault tolerance and consensus
  • QUANTUM — Post-quantum cryptography (Shor's algorithm, lattices)

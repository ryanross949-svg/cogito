About MALTHUS

Methodology and design principles.

MALTHUS is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the ecological and demographic thinking patterns used by systems thinkers, biologists, and macro-strategists. It is the darker, ecological counterpart to FERMI. It is not a textbook. It is a workout.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality. Forgetting curve combat.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition. You must produce the answer, not just identify it.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules. Forced discrimination strengthens memory vs. blocked practice.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project GAIA require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • ARITH — Exponential vs. linear growth (The Malthusian Trap)
  • CARRY — Carrying capacity and the logistic curve
  • OVERSHOOT — Exceeding limits and the mechanics of die-off
  • DELAY — Latency in system response and feedback
  • MINIMUM — Minimum viable population and the Allee effect
  • COMPETE — Competitive exclusion and niche overlap
  • PREDATE — Predator-prey dynamics and oscillation
  • ENERGY — Trophic levels, the 10% rule, and ecological footprint
  • RESILIENCE — Tipping points and alternative stable states
  • CIVIL — Civilizational limits and the Limits to Growth model

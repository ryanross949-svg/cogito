About DARWIN

Methodology and design principles.

DARWIN is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the strategic thinking patterns used in evolutionary biology. It trains you to view markets, organizations, and systems as evolving ecosystems governed by natural selection.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Biosphere require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • FITNESS — Natural selection, adaptation, survival of the fittest
  • VARIATION — Mutations, genetic diversity, the fuel of evolution
  • KINSHIP — Inclusive fitness, kin selection, Hamilton's Rule
  • SEXUAL — Sexual selection, costly signaling, honest indicators
  • COOPERATION — Reciprocal altruism, symbiosis, mutualism
  • ECOLOGY — Carrying capacity, niche construction, competition
  • SPECIATION — Reproductive isolation, punctuated equilibrium
  • EPIGENETICS — Gene expression, environmental triggers
  • SUPERORG — Eusociality, swarm intelligence, division of labor
  • REDQUEEN — Coevolution, arms races, host-parasite dynamics

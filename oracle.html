About ORACLE

Methodology and design principles.

ORACLE is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the strategic thinking patterns required to master AI tools, prompt engineering, and agentic workflows. It is not a tutorial. It is a workout.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality. Forgetting curve combat.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition. You must produce the answer, not just identify it.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules. Forced discrimination strengthens memory vs. blocked practice.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Singularity require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • CONTEXT — System priming, role prompting, and setting the stage
  • DECOMP — Chain of Thought (CoT) and breaking complex problems
  • GROUND — RAG, hallucination management, and anchoring to reality
  • FEWSHOT — Demonstrating patterns instead of giving instructions
  • TEMP — Tuning divergence vs. convergence (Temperature/Top-P)
  • ADVERSARY — Red-teaming, Socratic critique, and Devil's Advocate
  • STRUCTURE — JSON, XML, and programmatic output formatting
  • DELEGATE — Cognitive offloading and asymmetric task allocation
  • CHAIN — Agentic workflows and multi-step pipelines
  • CENTAUUR — Human-AI synthesis and competitive frontier execution

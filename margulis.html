About MARGULIS

Methodology and design principles.

MARGULIS is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the thinking patterns of Lynn Margulis and symbiogenesis. It challenges the pure "survival of the fittest/combat" narrative, teaching that the greatest leaps in complexity happen through permanent, structural mergers.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality. Forgetting curve combat.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition. You must produce the answer, not just identify it.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules. Forced discrimination strengthens memory vs. blocked practice.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project SYNERGY require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • MERGER — Endosymbiotic theory and cellular integration
  • FUSION — Structural mergers between distinct entities
  • TRANSFER — Horizontal gene transfer and genomic swapping
  • SYNERGY — Cooperation yielding emergent properties
  • PARASITE — The slippery slope from parasitism to mutualism
  • GENOME — Viral integration and mobile genetic elements
  • COMPLEXITY — Major transitions in evolution
  • NICHE — Niche construction and environmental engineering
  • SLIME — Biofilms, quorum sensing, and multicellularity
  • LEAP — Evolutionary jumps via mergers, not just mutation

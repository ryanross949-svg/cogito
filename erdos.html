About ERDOS

Methodology and design principles.

ERDOS is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the strategic thinking patterns used in network science, graph theory, and sociology. It trains you to understand how diseases, ideas, and failures spread through populations, power grids, and social structures.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project Cascade require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • DEGREES — Small-world networks, six degrees of separation, clustering
  • HUBS — Scale-free networks, power laws, preferential attachment
  • CONTAGION — Epidemiology, SIR models, R0, network diffusion
  • ROBUST — Percolation, resilience, targeted attacks vs random failures
  • CLUSTER — Community detection, modularity, echo chambers
  • CENTRAL — Betweenness, closeness, eigenvector centrality, PageRank
  • FLOW — Max-flow min-cut, bottlenecks, information capacity
  • SYNCHRONY — Coupled oscillators, Kuramoto model, consensus
  • DYNAMIC — Temporal networks, link prediction, evolving graphs
  • MULTIPLEX — Multi-layer networks, interdependency, cascading failures

About AXELROD

Methodology and design principles.

AXELROD is a cognitive training simulator designed to install the strategic thinking patterns of evolutionary cooperation, reciprocal altruism, and iterated game theory. It is not a sociology textbook. It is a workout.

// Core Pedagogy

The system uses four evidence-based learning mechanisms, applied in concert:

  • Spaced Repetition (SM-2): Cards reappear at expanding intervals based on recall quality. Forgetting curve combat.
  • Active Recall: Every drill requires generation, not recognition. You must produce the answer, not just identify it.
  • Interleaving: Drills mix concepts across modules. Forced discrimination strengthens memory vs. blocked practice.
  • Elaboration: Arena problems and Project ECHO require applying concepts to novel scenarios.

// The Ten Axes

  • SHADOW — The shadow of the future and iterated interactions
  • RECIP — Reciprocal altruism and vampire bats
  • TITFORTAT — The winning strategy of the iterated Prisoner\'s Dilemma
  • FORGIVE — Generosity and dealing with noise
  • CLUSTER — Spatial and network topology of cooperation
  • SIGNAL — Costly signaling and the green beard effect
  • NOISE — Errors, misperception, and Win-Stay Lose-Shift
  • POPULATION — Replicator dynamics and fitness landscapes
  • ESS — Evolutionary Stable Strategies
  • INSTITUTION — Solving the tragedy of the commons
